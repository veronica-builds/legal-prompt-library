# Contract Drafting Prompts

Prompts for generating first drafts, clauses, and contract templates.

---

## Generate a Full Contract First Draft

```
You are a senior commercial attorney with 20 years of experience. Draft a [CONTRACT TYPE]
between [PARTY A] ("Company") and [PARTY B] ("Vendor") governed by [STATE] law.

Key terms:
  - Scope of services: [DESCRIBE]
  - Contract value: [AMOUNT] payable [TERMS]
  - Term: [DURATION] with [RENEWAL]
  - Liability cap: [AMOUNT]

Include sections: Recitals, Definitions, Scope of Services, Compensation, Term and
Termination, Representations and Warranties, Indemnification, Limitation of Liability,
Confidentiality, Intellectual Property, Force Majeure, Dispute Resolution, General
Provisions.

Use formal legal conventions. Define all capitalized terms. Include cross-references.
Flag areas needing client-specific input with [ATTORNEY NOTE] annotations.
```

## Limitation of Liability Clause

```
Draft a limitation of liability clause for a SaaS agreement with the following terms:
- Liability cap: 12 months of fees paid in the prior 12-month period
- Exclude indirect, consequential, punitive, and incidental damages
- Carve out: fraud, gross negligence, willful misconduct, death/personal injury,
  indemnification obligations, and confidentiality breaches
- Mutual application to both parties

Governing law: [STATE].
```

## Indemnification Clause

Suggest a revised indemnity clause that is more balanced, limits our company's exposure,
and excludes indemnification for the other party's negligence or willful misconduct.

Current clause: [PASTE EXISTING CLAUSE]

We represent: [DESCRIBE YOUR CLIENT'S ROLE — vendor/customer/service provider]

## Assignment Prohibition Clause

```
Write a contract clause that:
- Prohibits assignment without prior written consent of the non-assigning party
- Voids any unauthorized assignment
- Explicitly addresses change-of-control events (acquisition, merger, IPO)
- Specifies whether consent may be unreasonably withheld

Governing law: [STATE].
```

*Source: Ten Things Blog*

## Work-for-Hire / IP Assignment (Contractor)

```
Draft a work-for-hire clause ensuring that all deliverables, inventions, and works
created by a contractor during the engagement are owned by our company, including:
- Assignment of all IP rights
- Waiver of moral rights to the extent permitted by law
- Obligation to execute further documents
- Disclosure requirement for inventions

Governing law: [STATE].
```

## Software License Clause

```
Write a license clause granting the customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable,
non-sublicensable license to use our software with the following limitations:
- Internal business use only
- Named user or site-based restrictions: [SPECIFY]
- No reverse engineering, decompilation, or derivative works
- Term coterminous with the agreement

Governing law: [STATE].
```

*Source: Ten Things Blog*

## Non-Disparagement Clause

```
Draft a non-disparagement clause for inclusion in a [settlement agreement / separation
agreement / vendor contract]. It should:
- Apply mutually to both parties
- Define the scope of prohibited statements
- Address social media and online reviews
- Include a carve-out for truthful statements in legal proceedings

Governing law: [STATE].
```

Use this to gather the information needed before drafting:

```
As experienced legal counsel, what information would you need to write a strong first draft
of a [SPECIFIC CONTRACT TYPE] in [STATE]?

List your questions organized by section of the agreement.
```

## Design Partner Agreement (1-Page)

```
Prepare a 1-page design partner agreement between a tech startup (providing IP and
technology) and a client (providing data and domain expertise). Cover:
- IP ownership and licensing
- Confidentiality obligations
- Commercial rights to jointly developed output
- Data usage rights and restrictions
- Term and termination

Keep it concise — no longer than one page. Governing law: [STATE].
```
