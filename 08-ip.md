```
(a) a utility patent, or
```

## Trademark Cease and Desist Letter

```
Draft a cease and desist letter for trademark infringement.

OUR CLIENT'S MARK:
- Mark: [MARK NAME / DESIGN]
- Registration number(s): [USPTO REG. NO.]
- Goods/services covered: [DESCRIBE]
- First use in commerce: [DATE]

INFRINGING ACTIVITY:
- Infringing mark: [DESCRIBE]
- Infringing goods/services: [DESCRIBE]
- Where encountered: [URL / LOCATION]
- Date discovered: [DATE]

DEMANDED ACTIONS:
- Immediately cease all use of the infringing mark
- Destroy or deliver up infringing materials
- Confirm compliance in writing by [DATE]

Reference: 15 U.S.C. § 1114 (registered marks) / § 1125(a) (unregistered marks).
Mention availability of statutory damages, attorney fees, and injunctive relief under
the Lanham Act. Maintain a professional tone.
```

## Work-for-Hire and IP Assignment Clause

```
Draft a comprehensive IP assignment and work-for-hire clause for inclusion in a
[contractor agreement / employment agreement / consulting agreement].

The clause should:
- Establish that all work product is a work made for hire under 17 U.S.C. § 101
- To the extent not a work-for-hire, assign all IP rights to the company
- Include a present-tense assignment (not merely a covenant to assign)
- Cover patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and moral rights
- Waive moral rights to the extent permitted by applicable law
- Require execution of further documents to perfect the assignment
- Address pre-existing IP (list and carve out with Schedule A)
- Cover IP developed using company resources or relating to company business

Governing law: [STATE].
```

```

relationships.

1. Pre-disclosure steps (NDA requirements, disclosure log)
2. Marking and labeling requirements
3. Access controls and need-to-know limitations
4. Employee and contractor training
5. Contractual protections (assignment, non-use, return/destruction)
6. Post-relationship obligations and enforcement
7. Documentation to support a trade secret misappropriation claim under DTSA

Company type: [DESCRIBE — what types of trade secrets you have]
```

## IP Due Diligence (Acquisition Target)

```
List key IP due diligence questions and document requests when acquiring a
[COMPANY TYPE — e.g., SaaS startup / biotech company / consumer brand].

Cover:
1. Patent portfolio (owned, licensed, pending, abandoned)
2. Trademark portfolio (registered, common law, international)
3. Copyright ownership (work-for-hire documentation, assignment records)
4. Trade secrets (what they are, how protected)
5. Open source usage and compliance
6. IP ownership by founders and key employees (assignment agreements)
7. Third-party IP used in the product (licenses, permissions)
8. IP litigation history and pending disputes
9. Government funding (Bayh-Dole implications)
10. Domain names and social media handles
```
