# Legal Operations Prompts

Prompts for managing a legal department — intake, matter tracking, KPIs, training, and working with outside counsel.

---

## Legal Intake Form Template

```
Create a legal intake form template for business teams to submit requests to the legal
department. The form should capture:

1. Requestor name, department, and contact information
2. Request type (contract review / new contract / compliance question / litigation /
   employment / other)
3. Brief description of the matter
4. Business urgency and requested completion date
5. Relevant deadlines (external — regulatory, contractual, court)
6. Counterparty name (if applicable)
7. Dollar value or business impact (if applicable)
8. Documents attached
9. Prior legal work on this matter (if any)
10. Preferred point of contact for follow-up

Keep it concise — no more than one page. Design for use in [email / Google Form / SharePoint].
```

## Guide for Business Teams: Working with Legal

```
Draft a short guide (1–2 pages) for business teams on how to work effectively with
the Legal department. Cover:

1. How to submit a legal request (intake process)
2. What information to include to get a faster response
3. Typical turnaround times by request type
4. When to loop in Legal early (before, not after, committing)
5. Common delays and how to avoid them
6. What Legal needs from you to do its job
7. What Legal cannot do (unauthorized practice, business decisions)

Tone: Friendly, practical, and non-condescending.
```

## Legal KPI Dashboard Framework

```
Draft a legal department KPI dashboard framework for tracking performance across:

1. Contract operations
   - Contract volume by type
   - Average turnaround time by contract type
   - Contracts exceeding SLA

2. Litigation
   - Active matters by status
   - Outside counsel spend YTD vs. budget
   - Matters resolved (settled / dismissed / won / lost)

3. Compliance
   - Training completion rates
   - Policy review status
   - Regulatory filings on time

4. Legal operations
   - Intake request volume by month
   - Average response time to business requests
   - Client satisfaction (if measured)

For each metric, specify: definition, data source, target, and reporting frequency.
```

## Outside Counsel Coordination Checklist

```
Create a checklist for coordinating with outside counsel on a new litigation matter.
Cover:

1. Engagement letter and scope confirmation
2. Budget: initial estimate, approval thresholds, update frequency
3. Billing guidelines (rate approval, staffing expectations, no block billing)
4. Litigation hold coordination
5. Case strategy alignment meeting
6. Communication protocols (who approves filings, response deadlines)
7. Regular status update schedule
8. Settlement authority and approval process
9. Document and data sharing procedures
10. Conflict check and co-counsel arrangements (if any)
```

## Attorney-Client Privilege Training Script

```
Write a script for a short (10-minute) employee training session on how to preserve
attorney-client privilege in written communications. Cover:

1. What attorney-client privilege is and why it matters
2. When communications with in-house counsel are (and are not) privileged
3. The #1 mistake employees make that waives privilege (copying non-legal personnel
   without necessity; forwarding legal advice without authorization)
4. How to properly mark privileged communications
5. What to do if you receive a litigation hold notice
6. Three real-world examples (anonymized) of privilege being waived

Tone: Engaging and practical, not legalistic. Include a 3-question quiz at the end.
```

## Common Privilege Waiver Mistakes

```
List the most common mistakes employees and managers make that risk waiving
attorney-client privilege. For each mistake:
- Describe the conduct
- Explain why it can waive privilege
- Provide a practical tip to avoid it

Cover at least:
- Forwarding legal advice to third parties
- Including unnecessary non-employee recipients on legal emails
- Discussing legal advice in non-privileged channels (Slack, public forums)
- Mixing business advice with legal advice in the same communication
- Disclosing privileged information during due diligence without a clawback agreement
```

## Compliance Policy Summary for Employees

```
Write a concise explanation of our [CODE OF CONDUCT / ANTI-BRIBERY POLICY /
CONFLICTS OF INTEREST POLICY] for use in new hire onboarding.

The summary should:
- Explain the purpose of the policy in plain language
- Cover the key rules employees must follow
- Give 2–3 concrete examples of do's and don'ts
- Explain how to report concerns (hotline, manager, legal)
- Describe protections against retaliation for good-faith reporting

Tone: Clear, direct, and approachable. Length: 1 page maximum.
```

## Conflict of Interest Policy and Disclosure Form

```
Write a sample conflict-of-interest policy and annual disclosure form for employees.

Policy should cover:
- Definition of a conflict of interest (actual, potential, and apparent)
- Examples of common conflicts (outside employment, investments, family relationships
  with vendors, board service)
- Disclosure obligation and timing
- Review process
- Consequences of non-disclosure

Disclosure form should ask:
- Employee name, title, department
- Whether any conflicts exist (yes/no with description)
- Attestation that employee has read and complied with the policy

Length: Policy — 1 page. Form — half page.
```
