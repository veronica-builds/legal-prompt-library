# Client Communications Prompts

Prompts for demand letters, client updates, intake emails, advisory letters, and cease and desist letters.

---

## Pre-Litigation Demand Letter

```
Draft a pre-litigation demand letter on behalf of [CLIENT NAME] to [RECIPIENT NAME/ENTITY].

FACTS:
- Dispute: [DESCRIBE]
- Key dates: [LIST]
- Damages: [DESCRIBE AND QUANTIFY]
- Prior attempts to resolve: [DESCRIBE OR "NONE"]

LEGAL BASIS:
- Primary claims: [LIST]
- Applicable law / jurisdiction: [STATE]

DEMANDED RELIEF:
- Specific demand: [AMOUNT OR ACTION]
- Response deadline: [DATE — typically 10–30 days]

The letter should:
1. State the facts clearly and accurately
2. Identify the legal claims with specificity
3. Articulate damages with supporting detail
4. State the demand and deadline
5. Reference willingness to litigate if necessary, professionally
6. Include a reservation of rights

Tone: Firm, professional, and factual. Not threatening or inflammatory.
```

## Enhanced Demand Letter (Highly Specific)

```
Act as an experienced commercial litigation attorney with expertise in [CLAIM TYPE]
in [STATE]. My client, [CLIENT NAME], entered into a [CONTRACT TYPE] with [OPPOSING PARTY]
on [DATE]. [OPPOSING PARTY] has [DESCRIBE BREACH / CONDUCT] despite [DESCRIBE PRIOR NOTICE
OR ATTEMPTS TO CURE].

Draft a formal demand letter requesting [SPECIFIC RELIEF]. The letter should be:
- Professionally formatted
- Approximately [500–800] words
- Cite relevant [STATE] [statutes / case law] supporting the claims
- Include standard legal notices and reservation of rights
- Assertive but not antagonistic
- Legally sound and persuasive without making unsupportable claims
```

## IP Cease and Desist Letter

```
Draft a cease and desist letter for [IP TYPE: trademark / copyright / patent / trade secret]
infringement.

OUR CLIENT'S IP:
- Description: [DESCRIBE IP]
- Registration numbers (if any): [LIST]
- First use date: [DATE]

INFRINGING ACTIVITY:
- Conduct: [DESCRIBE]
- Where occurring: [URL / LOCATION]
- Discovery date: [DATE]
- Evidence: [DESCRIBE]

DEMANDED ACTIONS:
- [SPECIFIC REQUIRED ACTIONS — e.g., cease all use, destroy infringing materials, provide
  accounting of profits]
- Response deadline: [DATE]

Include references to applicable federal/state IP statutes. Mention availability of
statutory damages and attorney fees. Maintain a professional tone that allows for
amicable resolution while demonstrating the strength of the position.
```

## Client Advisory Letter

```
Draft a client advisory letter for [CLIENT NAME], a [CLIENT TYPE — e.g., mid-size SaaS
company, individual entrepreneur], regarding [LEGAL ISSUE].

Context: [RELEVANT BACKGROUND — 2–3 sentences]

The letter should:
1. Open with a clear summary of the issue and our recommendation
2. Explain the relevant legal framework in plain English
3. Present available options with pros and cons of each
4. Include our recommended course of action with specific next steps
5. Note any time-sensitive deadlines
6. Close with a clear call to action

Tone: Professional but accessible for business executives.
Length: Maximum 1–2 pages.
```

## Case Status Update Email

```
Draft a case status update email for [CLIENT NAME] regarding [CASE NAME / MATTER].

Recent developments:
[LIST KEY EVENTS SINCE LAST UPDATE]

Upcoming deadlines:
[LIST WITH DATES]

Client action items (if any):
[LIST OR "NONE AT THIS TIME"]

The email should:
1. Lead with the most important recent development
2. Briefly explain what it means for the client's position
3. Clearly list upcoming deadlines and any client action required
4. Provide an honest assessment of where the case stands
5. Note any decisions the client needs to make

Length: Under 500 words. Use bullet points for action items.
Do not include legal citations unless necessary for client understanding.
```

*Source: The Legal Prompts / Clio*

## Initial Client Intake Email

```
Draft an initial consultation email for a [PRACTICE AREA] attorney.

The email should:
- Briefly introduce the attorney and firm
- Explain what to expect in the consultation
- Ask targeted questions to understand the client's situation and goals
- Request any relevant documents the client should bring or send in advance
- Set a professional and welcoming tone

Client type: [DESCRIBE IF KNOWN — e.g., individual, small business, startup]
Practice area: [E.g., employment, real estate, business litigation]
```

## Client Intake Questionnaire

```
Generate a client intake questionnaire for [TYPE OF LEGAL CASE — e.g., personal injury /
business formation / divorce / employment discrimination].

Include questions to gather:
- Basic contact and identifying information
- Background facts and timeline of events
- Relevant documents the client has in their possession
- Prior legal proceedings or claims related to this matter
- Goals and priorities for the representation
- Any deadlines or time-sensitive issues

Format as a fillable questionnaire with clear instructions.
```

## Client Engagement Letter

```
Generate a client engagement letter for [TYPE OF LEGAL SERVICE] with the following terms:

- Firm name: [NAME]
- Client name: [NAME]
- Scope of representation: [DESCRIBE]
- Fee arrangement: [HOURLY / FLAT FEE / CONTINGENCY — DESCRIBE TERMS]
- Billing cycle and payment terms: [DESCRIBE]
- Retainer amount (if any): [AMOUNT]
- Governing law: [STATE]
- Dispute resolution: [DESCRIBE]

Include:
- Scope of representation and any exclusions
- Client's obligations (cooperation, document production, payment)
- Termination provisions for both attorney and client
- Confidentiality and attorney-client privilege notice
- File retention policy
- Signature blocks

Comply with [STATE] Rules of Professional Conduct.
```

## Response to Cease and Desist

```
Draft a response to the following cease and desist letter received by my client, [CLIENT NAME].

Our position: [ACCEPTING / DISPUTING / PARTIALLY ACCEPTING] the allegations.

[PASTE CEASE AND DESIST LETTER]

The response should:
1. Acknowledge receipt without admitting liability
2. Respond to each allegation specifically
3. State our client's position clearly
4. If disputing: identify the factual and legal basis for our defense
5. If negotiating: propose a path to resolution
6. Reserve all rights

Tone: Professional and measured. Avoid escalating the dispute unnecessarily.
```

## Plain-English Explainer for Client

```
Draft a client-friendly explainer of [LEGAL DEVELOPMENT / COURT RULING / NEW REGULATION]:
- What changed or happened
- Who is affected and how
- Likely timelines and next steps
- What our client should do now

Include a short FAQ (3–5 questions) anticipating what the client will ask.
Tone: Clear, reassuring, and practical. Avoid legal jargon.
```
