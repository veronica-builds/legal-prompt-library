# Legal Research Prompts

Prompts for research memos, case law analysis, statutory interpretation, and jurisdiction comparisons.

> **Reminder:** Always independently verify citations. AI tools frequently hallucinate case names, docket numbers, and holdings. Use these prompts to generate frameworks and starting points — not final authorities.

---

## Full Legal Research Memorandum

```
You are a senior associate at an Am Law 100 firm drafting a legal research memorandum.

QUESTION PRESENTED: [STATE THE LEGAL QUESTION]
JURISDICTION: [STATE / FEDERAL / CIRCUIT]
RELEVANT FACTS: [SUMMARIZE KEY FACTS]

Provide:
1. Brief Answer (2–3 sentences)
2. Discussion organized by sub-issue, covering:
   - Statutory and regulatory framework
   - Leading cases and their holdings
   - Application to analogous facts
   - Circuit splits or unsettled areas of law
3. Practical recommendations for the client
4. Complete list of authorities cited (to be independently verified)

Use formal memo style. Clearly distinguish majority from minority positions.
```

## Statute Quick Sheet (Step-by-Step)

```
Interpret and summarize [STATUTE / RULE / REGULATION].

Step 1: Explain the statute's purpose and legislative history
Step 2: Define key terms as courts have interpreted them
Step 3: Identify deadlines, limitations periods, and procedural requirements
Step 4: Outline defenses and exceptions
Step 5: Cite controlling cases interpreting the statute
Step 6: Cross-reference related rules and regulations
```

## Identify Relevant Cases from Facts

```
Based on the following case facts, identify three to five past cases with similar facts
and legal issues. For each case:
- Summarize the court's decision and reasoning
- Note whether it was a favorable or unfavorable outcome for the plaintiff
- Identify the key legal points that make it analogous

Case facts: [SUMMARIZE FACTS]
Jurisdiction: [STATE / FEDERAL / CIRCUIT]
```

## Citation Audit Protocol

```
I will provide a list of legal authorities cited in a draft brief or memo. For each citation,
provide a verification workflow:

Step 1: Confirm the citation format is correct under [BLUEBOOK / LOCAL RULES]
Step 2: Identify any citations that cannot be independently verified and flag them
Step 3: Note any cases that appear to be superseded, overruled, or distinguished
Step 4: Flag any citations that appear potentially hallucinated or unverifiable

Citations to review:
[PASTE CITATION LIST]
```
